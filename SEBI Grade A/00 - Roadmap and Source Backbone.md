# 00 - Roadmap and Source Backbone

## Why This Chapter Matters

SEBI Grade A preparation must explain how markets fail and why regulation exists. Memorizing definitions of derivatives, mutual funds, or disclosure rules is not enough.

## The Big Picture

```text
market problem -> regulatory need -> SEBI mechanism -> impact on investors/companies/markets -> exam relevance
```

## First-Principles Explanation

Cause: securities markets need trust, disclosure, fair access, investor protection, and orderly conduct.

Mechanism: SEBI regulates issuers, intermediaries, market infrastructure, products, disclosures, enforcement, and investor protection.

Immediate result: market participants operate under rules for transparency and fairness.

Long-term impact: capital formation becomes more credible, but regulation must balance investor protection with market development.

Next connected topic: SEBI Act, Companies Act basics, primary market, secondary market, intermediaries, mutual funds, derivatives, bonds, finance, management, economics, and descriptive writing.

## Core Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary market | New securities issuance. | Capital raising and disclosure. |
| Secondary market | Trading existing securities. | Liquidity and price discovery. |
| Intermediary | Entity such as broker, merchant banker, RTA, advisor. | SEBI regulates conduct. |
| Derivative | Contract deriving value from underlying. | Risk transfer and speculation. |
| Mutual fund | Pooled investment vehicle. | Retail investor protection. |
| Disclosure | Required information to market/investors. | Reduces information asymmetry. |
| Enforcement | Regulatory action for violations. | Credibility of regulation. |

## Small Details That Matter Later

- SEBI Grade A stream-specific Paper 2 syllabus can differ by stream.

- Descriptive answers need investor/company/market impact, not only legal text.
- Primary and secondary market functions are different but connected.
- Derivatives questions often test margin, risk, payoff, and regulation.
- Current circulars and notifications need fresh verification before exam.

## Questions to Test Understanding

1. Why does securities regulation exist?
2. Why is disclosure central to capital markets?
3. Why are primary and secondary markets both important?
4. Why should regulations be explained through market problems?
5. Why do SEBI current affairs need close-to-exam updates?

## Answers and Reasoning

1. It protects investors, improves market integrity, reduces fraud, and supports capital formation.
2. Investors cannot price risk fairly without reliable information.
3. Primary markets raise capital; secondary markets provide liquidity and price discovery.
4. The exam tests regulatory purpose, not only section names.
5. Circulars, rules, enforcement, and market developments change frequently.
