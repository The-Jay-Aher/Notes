## High-Yield Exam Notes

- Environmental issues arise when population growth, consumption, industrialisation, and urbanisation exceed the carrying capacity of natural systems.
- Air pollution control requires reducing particulates, oxides of nitrogen/sulphur, vehicular emissions, industrial emissions, and indoor pollution.
- Water pollution includes sewage, industrial effluents, agrochemicals, nutrient enrichment, and biological contamination.
- Solid waste management requires segregation, reuse, recycling, composting, sanitary disposal, and reduced generation.
- Greenhouse effect and global warming link static ecology with climate-change current affairs.
- Ozone depletion is linked with CFCs and international action such as the Montreal Protocol.
- Deforestation creates biodiversity loss, soil erosion, floods, climate impacts, and livelihood stress.
- UPSC angle: CPCB, pollution control laws, climate governance, waste management, biodiversity conservation, and community movements such as Chipko.
