## High-Yield Exam Notes

- A resource is useful only when it is technologically accessible, economically feasible, and culturally acceptable.
- Resources can be classified by origin, exhaustibility, ownership, and development status.
- Resource planning means identifying, mapping, evaluating, and matching resources with technology, institutions, and development priorities.
- Sustainable development requires present use without destroying the ability of future generations to meet their needs.
- Rio Earth Summit and Agenda 21 are high-yield environment-development references from the chapter.
- Land-use patterns reflect relief, climate, soil, population, technology, and institutional choices.
- Land degradation and soil erosion are important Prelims and Mains themes; connect them with mining, overgrazing, deforestation, irrigation, and conservation.
- Map practice: soil distribution, land-use patterns, degraded regions, and conservation methods.
