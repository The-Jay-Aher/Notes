## High-Yield Exam Notes

- A Constitution creates the basic rules of political life and defines who can exercise power and how.
- It limits arbitrary power, protects rights, allocates authority, and provides a shared framework for social cooperation.
- The Indian Constitution emerged through the Constituent Assembly and reflected debates on liberty, equality, justice, representation, and national unity.
- Constitutions are not only legal documents; they express political values and institutional arrangements.
- UPSC angle: connect this chapter with Preamble, constitutional morality, rule of law, limited government, and legitimacy.
- Prelims focus: Constituent Assembly chronology, constitutional functions, and basic values.
