## Quick Summary

- UPSC GS I world history starts from the eighteenth century and includes Industrial Revolution, world wars, redrawing of boundaries, colonization, decolonization, and political philosophies such as communism, capitalism, and socialism.
- The target is not specialist history; it is cause-effect analysis and society impact.
- Use world history to enrich Essay, IR, political ideology, and society answers.

## Official Source Anchors

- UPSC explicitly lists events from the eighteenth century: Industrial Revolution, world wars, national boundary changes, colonization, decolonization, political philosophies, and their effect on society.
- Existing Class 10 history notes already cover nationalism in Europe, global world, and industrialization as a base.

## High-Yield Exam Notes

## Event: Industrial Revolution

## Time period:
- Began in Britain in the eighteenth century; spread unevenly to Europe, North America, and later other regions.

## Causes:
- Capital accumulation, coal and iron resources, agricultural change, colonial markets, technological innovation, labour availability, transport improvements, institutional support.

## Key people:
- Inventors, entrepreneurs, workers, factory owners, reformers; do not over-focus on names unless asked.

## Impact:
- Factory system, urbanization, capitalism, labour exploitation, class formation, imperial expansion, environmental change, transport revolution.

## UPSC angle:
- Link Industrial Revolution with colonialism, capitalism, labour movements, urban problems, and climate roots.

## Event: American And French Revolutions

## Time period:
- Late eighteenth century.

## Causes:
- Enlightenment ideas, taxation, representation demands, class tensions, absolutism, colonial grievances.

## Key people:
- American revolutionaries, French revolutionaries, philosophers, middle classes, peasants, workers.

## Impact:
- Popular sovereignty, rights, constitutionalism, republicanism, nationalism, modern citizenship.

## UPSC angle:
- Use for democracy, rights, constitutionalism, and political modernity.

## Event: Nationalism And Nation-State Formation

## Time period:
- Nineteenth century onward.

## Causes:
- Common language/culture claims, print capitalism, industrialization, wars, liberal ideas, state centralization.

## Key people:
- Nationalist leaders in Europe and colonies; unification figures in Italy and Germany.

## Impact:
- Modern nation-states, redrawn boundaries, minority questions, wars, citizenship debates.

## UPSC angle:
- Connect with Indian nationalism and post-colonial nation-building.

## Event: Imperialism And Colonialism

## Time period:
- Intensified from eighteenth to twentieth century.

## Causes:
- Industrial raw material needs, markets, strategic control, missionary activity, racial ideology, military technology, finance capital.

## Key people:
- Colonial states, companies, settlers, local intermediaries, resistance movements.

## Impact:
- Economic extraction, cultural change, racial hierarchy, plantation economies, new borders, infrastructure for extraction, anti-colonial nationalism.

## UPSC angle:
- Use as a framework for Indian colonial economy and global south underdevelopment.

## Event: World War I

## Time period:
- 1914-1918.

## Causes:
- Militarism, alliances, imperial competition, nationalism, Balkan tensions, assassination crisis.

## Key people:
- European powers, Ottoman Empire, soldiers from colonies, US entry, revolutionary Russia.

## Impact:
- Collapse of empires, Treaty of Versailles, League of Nations, redrawn boundaries, economic stress, conditions for World War II.

## UPSC angle:
- Link with nationalism, colonial soldiers, Middle East boundaries, Russian Revolution, and inter-war crisis.

## Event: Russian Revolution And Communism

## Time period:
- 1917 and aftermath.

## Causes:
- Tsarist autocracy, war stress, inequality, peasant distress, worker mobilisation, revolutionary ideology.

## Key people:
- Lenin, Bolsheviks, workers, soldiers, peasants.

## Impact:
- First socialist state, planned economy, communist movements, ideological conflict, Cold War roots.

## UPSC angle:
- Compare communism, socialism, capitalism, and welfare-state responses.

## Event: World War II

## Time period:
- 1939-1945.

## Causes:
- Treaty of Versailles grievances, fascism, Nazism, militarism, appeasement, imperial ambitions, global depression.

## Key people:
- Allied and Axis powers, resistance movements, colonial soldiers, civilians.

## Impact:
- United Nations, decolonization acceleration, Cold War, human rights regime, nuclear age, boundary changes.

## UPSC angle:
- Link with UN, decolonization, India, human rights, and modern global order.

## Event: Decolonization

## Time period:
- Mainly after World War II, with earlier anti-colonial roots.

## Causes:
- Colonial exploitation, nationalist movements, war weakening of Europe, global anti-imperial sentiment, UN principles, mass mobilisation.

## Key people:
- Anti-colonial leaders in Asia, Africa, Latin America; nationalist parties; workers and peasants.

## Impact:
- New nation-states, boundary conflicts, development challenges, non-alignment, post-colonial identity, state-building problems.

## UPSC angle:
- Compare India with Africa and Southeast Asia; link with Non-Aligned Movement and development state.

## Prelims Pointers

- World history is mainly Mains, but Prelims can ask world events through art, culture, economy, geography, or IR.
- Know basic chronology: Industrial Revolution, American/French revolutions, nationalism, imperialism, World War I, Russian Revolution, Great Depression, World War II, Cold War, decolonization.

## Mains Pointers

- Write causes and consequences in clusters: political, economic, social, ideological, international.
- Avoid excessive names and battle details.
- Use maps mentally: Europe, colonies, Middle East, Africa, major trade routes.
- Link world history with Indian nationalism and current IR where relevant.

## Possible UPSC Questions

- Mains-style: How did the Industrial Revolution transform society and imperial expansion?
- Mains-style: Explain how World War I created conditions for World War II.
- Mains-style: Discuss the role of nationalism in both liberation and conflict.
- Mains-style: Compare decolonization in Asia and Africa.
- Mains-style: Analyse the social effects of capitalism and socialism in the modern world.

## Things To Revise Repeatedly

- Causes of Industrial Revolution.
- Nationalism and imperialism.
- World War I causes and consequences.
- Russian Revolution and communism.
- World War II and the UN.
- Decolonization and post-colonial state-building.

## Revision Summary

- UPSC world history is cause-effect and society impact.
- Industrial Revolution created modern capitalism, labour politics, urbanization, and imperial expansion.
- World wars reshaped boundaries, institutions, and ideologies.
- Colonization and decolonization explain many modern development and border issues.
- Ideologies should be understood by their social effects, not only definitions.
