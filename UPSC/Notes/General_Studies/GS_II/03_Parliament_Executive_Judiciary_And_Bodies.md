## Quick Summary

- GS II requires institution-wise clarity: structure, appointment, powers, functions, accountability, limitations, and reform.
- The Representation of the People Act, 1951 is specifically mentioned in UPSC syllabus through "salient features of the Representation of People's Act".
- For Prelims, make body-wise tables. For Mains, explain institutional role in democracy, checks and balances, and public accountability.

## Source Anchors

- India Code records the Representation of the People Act, 1951 as the law for conduct of elections, qualifications and disqualifications, corrupt practices, offences, and election disputes.
- Official Constitution source should be used for exact Article references.
- Existing NCERT polity notes cover Parliament, executive, judiciary, election, and representation basics.
