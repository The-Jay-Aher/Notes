## Quick Summary

- International Relations in GS II is India-centric: India's interests, neighbours, regional groupings, global institutions, developed/developing country policies, diaspora, and strategic/economic effects.
- Use MEA releases, Parliament answers, official joint statements, and credible reports for current examples.
- Good answers balance interests, values, constraints, geography, economics, security, diaspora, and multilateralism.

## Source Anchors

- MEA Parliament answer on Act East states that ASEAN is at the core of the policy and that the policy promotes economic cooperation, cultural ties, strategic relationships, and broad connectivity in the Indo-Pacific.
- MEA official material describes Neighbourhood First as focusing on immediate neighbours with physical, digital, trade, commerce, and people-to-people connectivity.
- UPSC syllabus includes neighbourhood relations, bilateral/regional/global groupings, policies of developed/developing countries, diaspora, and international institutions.

## High-Yield Exam Notes

## Topic: India And Its Neighbourhood

## Meaning:
- India's relations with immediate neighbours: Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Bhutan, Maldives, Myanmar, Nepal, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, and the wider Indian Ocean neighbourhood.

## Constitutional link:
- Foreign policy is not directly a single article topic for GS answers; connect with sovereignty, security, development, peace, and Directive Principle ideals where relevant.

## Important institutions:
- MEA, PMO, Ministry of Defence, Commerce Ministry, border agencies, BIMSTEC, SAARC, BBIN, IORA.

## Current example:
- Use MEA releases or Parliament answers for current developments.

## Possible Mains question:
- Neighbourhood First is essential for India's security and development. Discuss.

## Topic: Act East And Indo-Pacific

## Meaning:
- Policy to strengthen ties with Southeast Asia and the wider Indo-Pacific through economic, cultural, strategic, and connectivity engagement.

## Important institutions:
- ASEAN, East Asia Summit, ADMM-Plus, ASEAN Regional Forum, QUAD, BIMSTEC.

## Current example:
- Use official MEA sources for projects, summits, and statements.

## Possible Mains question:
- Discuss the strategic and economic importance of India's Act East Policy.

## Prelims Pointers

- Prelims can ask membership, headquarters, reports, initiatives, declarations, and grouping composition.
- Track India-related organizations: BIMSTEC, ASEAN, SCO, BRICS, QUAD, IORA, ISA, CDRI, G20.
- Verify membership and current chairmanship close to exam.

## Mains Pointers

- Use an India-interest frame: security, economy, diaspora, technology, energy, climate, values, and strategic autonomy.
- Add geography: borders, sea lanes, Indian Ocean, Himalayas, Indo-Pacific, Central Asia.
- Avoid emotional language; write in interests, constraints, and policy options.
- Current examples must come from MEA/joint statements/reliable reports.
