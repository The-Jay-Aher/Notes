## Quick Summary

- Social justice in GS II covers vulnerable sections, welfare schemes, laws, institutions, health, education, human resources, poverty, hunger, and scheme performance.
- Good answers require constitutional values, beneficiary perspective, implementation challenges, and measurable outcomes.
- Use current data only after verification from official sources such as Census, NFHS, PLFS, government ministries, NITI Aayog, or official reports.

## High-Yield Exam Notes

## Topic: Social Justice

## Meaning:
- Fair access to rights, resources, opportunities, dignity, and public services, especially for disadvantaged groups.

## Constitutional link:
- Preamble justice and equality, Fundamental Rights, Directive Principles, reservations, welfare state, dignity, non-discrimination.

## Important articles/institutions:
- Ministries and commissions for SC, ST, OBC, minorities, women, children, persons with disabilities, elderly; courts; local bodies.

## Current example:
- Current example: To be updated from official scheme data.

## Possible Mains question:
- Social justice requires both legal protection and capability expansion. Discuss.

## Topic: Vulnerable Sections

## Meaning:
- Groups facing structural disadvantage, exclusion, violence, poverty, disability, age-related vulnerability, or reduced access to services.

## Important groups:
- Women, children, elderly, persons with disabilities, SCs, STs, OBCs, minorities, migrants, informal workers, LGBTQIA+ persons, homeless persons, disaster-affected groups.

## Constitutional link:
- Equality, non-discrimination, special provisions, social welfare, dignity, right to life, education.

## Current example:
- Current example: To be updated from official reports.

## Possible Mains question:
- Explain why targeted welfare is necessary in a formally equal democracy.

## Topic: Welfare Schemes

## Meaning:
- Government programmes for welfare, empowerment, protection, service delivery, and inclusion.

## Evaluation framework:
- Objective, target group, ministry, funding, delivery mechanism, coverage, exclusion errors, leakages, outcomes, grievance redress, monitoring.

## Constitutional link:
- Directive Principles, equality, dignity, welfare state.

## Current example:
- Use only scheme details from official ministry portals or budget documents.

## Possible Mains question:
- Discuss the design and implementation challenges in welfare schemes for vulnerable sections.

## Topic: Health

## Meaning:
- Physical, mental, and social well-being supported by public health systems, preventive care, treatment, nutrition, sanitation, and health finance.

## Important dimensions:
- Primary healthcare, public health, disease surveillance, health insurance, nutrition, mental health, maternal-child health, human resources, federal coordination.

## Constitutional link:
- Right to life, Directive Principles, local governance, social justice.

## Current example:
- Use verified health data from NFHS, Health Ministry, WHO, or official reports.

## Possible Mains question:
- Strengthening primary healthcare is essential for both equity and efficiency. Discuss.

## Topic: Education And Human Resources

## Meaning:
- Education builds capability, citizenship, productivity, social mobility, and democratic participation.

## Important dimensions:
- Access, quality, equity, foundational learning, digital divide, teacher capacity, higher education, skilling, vocational education, inclusion.

## Constitutional link:
- Right to education, equality, Directive Principles, human dignity.

## Current example:
- Use official education surveys and ministry data after verification.

## Possible Mains question:
- Education policy must focus on learning outcomes, not only enrolment. Discuss.

## Topic: Poverty

## Meaning:
- Deprivation in income, nutrition, health, education, shelter, security, dignity, and opportunity.

## Important dimensions:
- Multidimensional poverty, regional inequality, social exclusion, employment, inflation, welfare delivery, migration.

## Constitutional link:
- Social and economic justice, Directive Principles, welfare schemes.

## Current example:
- Use official poverty and multidimensional poverty data after verification.

## Possible Mains question:
- Poverty reduction requires growth, redistribution, and public services. Analyse.

## Topic: Hunger And Nutrition

## Meaning:
- Lack of adequate, safe, nutritious food and the conditions needed for absorption, such as health, sanitation, and care.

## Important dimensions:
- Food security, PDS, ICDS, school meals, maternal nutrition, child stunting, wasting, anaemia, agriculture, water, sanitation.

## Constitutional link:
- Right to life, Directive Principles, food security law, welfare state.

## Current example:
- Use NFHS, government nutrition dashboards, and official reports after verification.

## Possible Mains question:
- Hunger is not only a food-supply problem but also a health, sanitation, and social equity problem. Discuss.

## Prelims Pointers

- Prelims may ask schemes, ministries, target groups, constitutional provisions, reports, and indexes.
- Do not memorize schemes without ministry, objective, beneficiary, and funding pattern.
- Verify current scheme names and mergers before exam.

## Mains Pointers

- Use beneficiary-first analysis.
- Add implementation problems: awareness, access, exclusion, leakage, capacity, data errors, federal coordination.
- Add reforms: convergence, local bodies, social audit, portability, DBT where suitable, grievance redress, outcome measurement.
- Use constitutional values in conclusion.
