## Quick Summary

- This topic is the spine of GS II. It links the freedom movement, Constituent Assembly, constitutional values, institutional design, rights, federalism, amendments, judicial review, and the basic structure doctrine.
- For Prelims, focus on constitutional terms, bodies, amendments, schedules, articles, and institutional functions.
- For Mains, focus on constitutional morality, checks and balances, federalism, rights, social justice, and institutional reform.

## High-Yield Exam Notes

## Topic: Historical Underpinnings

## Meaning:
- The Constitution emerged from colonial constitutional reforms, nationalist demands, mass politics, social reform, and the Constituent Assembly.

## Constitutional link:
- The Constitution transformed anti-colonial demands into a democratic republic with rights, representative institutions, federalism, judicial review, and social justice commitments.

## Important articles/institutions:
- Constituent Assembly, Preamble, Fundamental Rights, Directive Principles, Parliament, judiciary, federal structure, Election Commission.

## Meaning:
- Core design principles that make the Constitution work as a political and legal framework.

## Constitutional link:
- Sovereign democratic republic, parliamentary system, federalism with unitary features, Fundamental Rights, Directive Principles, independent judiciary, judicial review, universal adult franchise, constitutional bodies, emergency provisions.

## Topic: Preamble And Constitutional Values

## Meaning:
- The Preamble states the philosophical foundation of the Constitution.

## Constitutional link:
- Justice, liberty, equality, fraternity, dignity, unity and integrity guide interpretation and policy goals.

## Important articles/institutions:
- Preamble; Fundamental Rights; Directive Principles; Fundamental Duties.

## Current example:
- Use verified judgments or debates where courts or institutions refer to constitutional values.

## Possible Mains question:
- How do Preamble values guide governance in a diverse democracy?

## Topic: Fundamental Rights

## Meaning:
- Enforceable rights that protect individuals and groups against arbitrary state action and sometimes against private social power.

## Constitutional link:
- Equality, freedoms, protection against exploitation, freedom of religion, cultural and educational rights, constitutional remedies.

## Important articles/institutions:
- Supreme Court, High Courts, writ jurisdiction, judicial review, legal aid institutions.

## Current example:
- Current example: To be updated from current affairs.

## Possible Mains question:
- Fundamental Rights are not absolute but their restrictions must be constitutional. Discuss.

## Topic: Directive Principles And Social Revolution

## Meaning:
- Non-justiciable governance principles that guide the state toward social and economic democracy.

## Constitutional link:
- Welfare state, equitable distribution, health, education, work, justice, local governance, environment, international peace.

## Important articles/institutions:
- Parliament, State legislatures, governments, courts in interpretive use, welfare bodies.

## Current example:
- Use verified welfare-law or policy examples.

## Possible Mains question:
- Discuss the relationship between Fundamental Rights and Directive Principles in India's constitutional development.

## Topic: Basic Structure Doctrine

## Meaning:
- Judicial principle that Parliament can amend the Constitution but cannot destroy its basic structure.

## Constitutional link:
- Constitutional supremacy, limited amending power, judicial review, democracy, federalism, rule of law, secularism, separation of powers.

## Important articles/institutions:
- Parliament, Supreme Court, constitutional amendment process.

## Prelims Pointers

- Revise Preamble terms, Fundamental Rights, Directive Principles, Fundamental Duties, schedules, amendment procedure, emergency provisions.

## Mains Pointers

- Start Constitution answers with value or design logic, not only Article numbers.
- Use structure: constitutional basis, issue, current challenge, judicial/institutional interpretation, reform.
- Add exact Article numbers only after verifying from the Constitution.
