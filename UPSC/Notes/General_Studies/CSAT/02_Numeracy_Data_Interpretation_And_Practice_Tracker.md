- Keep a weekly timed practice tracker; do not postpone CSAT until the final month.

## Topic: Data Interpretation

## Areas:
- Tables.
- Bar charts.
- Pie charts.
- Line graphs.
- Mixed graphs.
- Caselets.
- Data sufficiency.

## Method:
1. Read title and units.
2. Note base year or total.
3. Identify what is asked.
4. Approximate if options allow.
5. Watch percentages of percentages.
6. Avoid assuming missing data.

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing percentage-point change with percent change.
- Ignoring units.
- Taking average of percentages incorrectly.
- Misreading graph scale.
- Spending too long on one set.
- Arithmetic slips in simple operations.

## Formula Sheet

## Percentage:
- Percent change = change / original x 100.

## Average:
- Average = sum / number of items.

## Speed:
- Speed = distance / time.

## Work:
- If A finishes work in x days, A's one-day work is 1/x.

## Simple interest:
- SI = principal x rate x time / 100.

## Compound interest:
- Amount = principal x (1 + rate/100)^time.

## Ratio:
- If a:b and total is T, a share = a/(a+b) x T.
