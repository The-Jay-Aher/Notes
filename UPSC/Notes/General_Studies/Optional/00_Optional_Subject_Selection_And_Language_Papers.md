## Quick Summary

- The vault cannot honestly create detailed optional notes until the optional subject is chosen.
- This note records the preparation workflow and boundary for optional and qualifying language papers.
- Once the optional is selected, create two subject-specific folders: `Optional/<Subject>/Paper_I/` and `Optional/<Subject>/Paper_II/`.

## Official Boundary

## Optional papers:
- Paper VI: Optional Subject Paper I.
- Paper VII: Optional Subject Paper II.
- Each paper carries 250 marks.
- The optional syllabus is broadly honours-degree level, with exceptions where the notification specifies another standard.

## Qualifying papers:
- Paper A: one Indian language from the Eighth Schedule, where applicable.
- Paper B: English.
- These are qualifying papers; marks are not counted for ranking if qualifying standards are met.

## Optional Selection Criteria

## Choose based on:
- Genuine interest.
- Overlap with GS.
- Availability of standard sources.
- Availability of previous-year questions.
- Ability to write analytical answers.
- Syllabus length and clarity.
- Graduation background, if useful.
- Time available.

## Do not choose based only on:
- Rumoured scoring trend.
- Toppers' optional without fit.
- Coaching availability alone.
- Short-term popularity.

## Paper I Workflow

1. Copy exact official syllabus lines.
2. Group topics into 8-12 modules.
3. Add standard source list.
4. Create concept notes.
5. Attach PYQs under each topic.
6. Create model answer skeletons.
7. Revise diagrams, thinkers, cases, and examples.

## Paper II Workflow

1. Copy exact official syllabus lines.
2. Separate static theory from applied/current topics.
3. Add India-specific examples where relevant.
4. Track case studies and reports.
5. Practice answer writing weekly.

## Language Paper Workflow

## English:
- Comprehension.
- Precis writing.
- Usage and vocabulary.
- Short essays.

## Indian language:
- Comprehension.
- Precis writing.
- Usage and vocabulary.
- Short essays.
- Translation from English to Indian language and vice versa.

## Practice Method:
- One precis every week.
- One translation set every week for chosen language.
- Vocabulary list from mistakes.
- Timed practice under qualifying-paper conditions.

## Risk Notes

- Optional is high weight; weak source selection can damage the attempt.
- Language papers are qualifying but cannot be ignored.
- Optional answer writing must be different from GS: more depth, terminology, and subject-specific structure.

## Prelims Pointers

- Optional does not affect Prelims directly, but optional choice affects time allocation before Prelims.

## Mains Pointers

- Optional requires PYQ-first planning.
- The first deliverable after optional selection should be a topic-wise syllabus map.
- Language practice should start early if the chosen language is not used regularly.
