## Quick Summary

- Language papers are qualifying, but failure can stop evaluation of merit papers.
- Prepare them with timed practice, especially precis, comprehension, grammar, short essays, and translation where applicable.
- This note gives a language-neutral practice system until the specific Indian language is chosen.

## English Paper Practice

## Comprehension:
- Read the passage twice.
- Underline thesis, reasons, examples, and conclusion.
- Answer only from the passage.
- Avoid outside knowledge.

## Precis:
- Identify central idea.
- Remove examples and repetition.
- Keep logical order.
- Use your own words.
- Stay within word limit.
- Do not add opinion.

## Usage and vocabulary:
- Maintain an error list for grammar, prepositions, articles, tense, word choice, and sentence clarity.

## Short essay:
- Use a simple structure: introduction, three arguments, counterpoint, conclusion.
- Write clearly rather than ornamentally.

## Indian Language Paper Practice

## Comprehension:
- Practise formal prose, newspaper editorials, government notices, and literary prose if relevant.

## Precis:
- Focus on concise expression in the chosen language.
- Avoid direct English sentence structure if it sounds unnatural.

## Translation:
- English to Indian language: preserve meaning, tone, and institutional vocabulary.
- Indian language to English: use simple, accurate English.

## Vocabulary:
- Build a list for governance, economy, society, environment, science, and ethics terms.

## Common Mistakes

- Ignoring language papers because they are qualifying.
- Writing long, decorative essays.
- Adding personal opinion in precis.
- Translating word-for-word.
- Weak handwriting or poor paragraphing.
- Not practising under time limits.

## Prelims Pointers

- Language papers do not affect Prelims, but weak language skills also hurt CSAT comprehension and Mains expression.

## Mains Pointers

- Qualifying papers should be made safe before Mains month.
- Language practice can improve Essay, GS, and optional expression.
- Keep one notebook for corrected sentences and vocabulary.
