## Quick Summary

- UPSC optional preparation cannot be completed generically because the candidate chooses one subject.
- This catalogue records the official optional universe and gives a preparation map for whichever optional is selected.
- Use this file to choose the optional, then create detailed subject notes only for that selected optional.

## How To Choose Optional

## Step 1: Interest test
- Can you read the subject for 8-12 months without resentment?
- Can you revise the same thinkers, theories, maps, cases, or formulas repeatedly?

## Step 2: Source test
- Are standard books and official syllabus explanations available?
- Are previous-year question papers available from UPSC?
- Can you obtain answer feedback?

## Step 3: Answer-writing test
- Can you write 150-250 word answers with subject-specific vocabulary?
- Can you add diagrams, thinkers, maps, cases, laws, or formulas where required?

## Step 4: Overlap test
- Does the subject support GS, Essay, interview, or current affairs?
- Overlap is useful, but it should not override interest and answer-writing fit.

## Step 5: Risk test
- Is the syllabus too technical for your background?
- Is the source list too scattered?
- Is there high dependence on coaching notes without primary texts?

## Optional PYQ Workflow

1. Download official previous-year optional papers from the UPSC previous-question page.
2. Tag each question to syllabus line.
3. Mark repeated themes.
4. Prepare answer skeletons.
5. Add value additions: thinkers, diagrams, maps, examples, cases, data, judgments, formulas, or quotations depending on subject.
6. Revise weak modules more often than familiar modules.

## Prelims Pointers

- Optional does not directly affect Prelims, but optional reading can support Essay and GS.
- Do not let optional consume the last Prelims revision window.

## Mains Pointers

- Optional has 500 merit marks; it needs answer-writing practice, not just reading.
- Paper I is often theory-heavy; Paper II often needs application, examples, and current linkage depending on subject.
- Make the first chosen-optional deliverable a syllabus-to-PYQ map.
