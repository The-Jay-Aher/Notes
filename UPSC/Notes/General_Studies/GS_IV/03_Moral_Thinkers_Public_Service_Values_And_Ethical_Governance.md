## Quick Summary

- Moral thinkers provide vocabulary for ethical reasoning, but UPSC rewards application more than name-dropping.
- Public service values convert constitutional ideals into conduct: fairness, duty, transparency, accountability, compassion, and restraint.
- Ethical governance requires institutions, incentives, transparency, and leadership, not only personal virtue.

## High-Yield Exam Notes

## Topic: Moral Thinkers From India

## Mahatma Gandhi:
- Core ideas: truth, non-violence, trusteeship, means-end purity, sarvodaya, antyodaya.
- Administrative use: non-violent conflict resolution, integrity, service to the weakest, ethical public life.

## B.R. Ambedkar:
- Core ideas: constitutional morality, social democracy, liberty, equality, fraternity, annihilation of caste, rule of law.
- Administrative use: rights-based governance, dignity, anti-discrimination, institutional morality.

## Swami Vivekananda:
- Core ideas: service, character, strength, dignity of human being, practical spirituality.
- Administrative use: citizen dignity, youth motivation, service orientation.

## Buddha:
- Core ideas: compassion, middle path, mindfulness, non-harm, self-discipline.
- Administrative use: restraint, empathy, conflict reduction, moderation.

## Kautilya:
- Core ideas: statecraft, welfare of subjects, discipline, anti-corruption vigilance, practical governance.
- Administrative use: public finance discipline, accountability, institutional control.

## Guru Nanak:
- Core ideas: equality, service, honest work, community, rejection of hierarchy.
- Administrative use: inclusion, social harmony, dignity.

## Savitribai Phule:
- Core ideas: education, gender justice, anti-caste reform, social courage.
- Administrative use: inclusive education, reform leadership, dignity of marginalized groups.

## Topic: Moral Thinkers From The World

## Aristotle:
- Virtue ethics, golden mean, character formation.
- Use: civil servants need cultivated habits, not only rules.

## Immanuel Kant:
- Duty, categorical imperative, treating persons as ends.
- Use: do not use citizens as instruments for targets or political gain.

## John Stuart Mill:
- Utilitarianism, greatest good, liberty principle.
- Use: evaluate consequences while protecting individual liberty.

## John Rawls:
- Justice as fairness, veil of ignorance, priority to basic liberties and least advantaged.
- Use: policy design for vulnerable groups.

## Nelson Mandela:
- Reconciliation, courage, forgiveness with justice.
- Use: conflict resolution and democratic leadership.

## Martin Luther King Jr.:
- Non-violent resistance, justice, equality, moral courage.
- Use: rights-based social change.

## Topic: Public Service Values

## Meaning:
- Values that guide the use of public authority and public resources.

## Core values:
- Integrity.
- Accountability.
- Transparency.
- Impartiality.
- Non-partisanship.
- Objectivity.
- Responsiveness.
- Compassion.
- Efficiency.
- Rule of law.
- Public interest.

## Status and problems:
- Status: civil services operate under constitutional values, laws, conduct norms, audit, courts, legislature, media, and citizens.
- Problems: corruption, politicization, delay, opacity, conflict of interest, discretion without accountability, weak grievance redressal, poor work culture.

## Topic: Ethical Concerns And Dilemmas In Government

## Common dilemmas:
- Political direction versus legal duty.
- Public disclosure versus confidentiality.
- Procurement speed versus due process.
- Welfare inclusion versus fraud prevention.
- Transfer pressure versus independent decision.
- Security secrecy versus public accountability.

## Ethical response:
- Verify law and facts.
- Record reasons.
- Escalate when required.
- Protect vulnerable groups.
- Refuse unlawful instructions.
- Maintain procedural fairness.
- Enable grievance redressal.

## Topic: Ethical Concerns In Private Institutions

## Areas:
- Corporate governance.
- Consumer protection.
- Data privacy.
- Labour rights.
- Environmental compliance.
- Tax avoidance.
- Misleading advertising.
- Financial fraud.

## Public administration link:
- Regulators must balance innovation, investment, consumer welfare, labour, environment, and fair competition.

## Topic: Laws, Rules, Regulations And Conscience

## Laws:
- Formal rules made by competent authority; provide legitimacy and predictability.

## Rules and regulations:
- Operationalize laws and guide daily conduct.

## Conscience:
- Inner moral judgement.

## Balance:
- Conscience cannot justify breaking law casually.
- If a legal order is unethical or unlawful, use institutional remedies, written dissent, appeal, whistleblowing protections where applicable, or refusal of illegal orders.

## Topic: Accountability And Ethical Governance

## Accountability types:
- Legal: courts and law.
- Administrative: hierarchy, review, audit.
- Legislative: committees, questions, budget oversight.
- Social: media, civil society, citizen feedback.
- Financial: audit, expenditure rules.
- Professional: codes, conduct, service norms.

## Ethical governance tools:
- Transparency.
- RTI.
- Citizen charters.
- Social audit.
- Grievance redressal.
- E-procurement.
- Audit.
- Whistleblower protection.
- Conflict-of-interest rules.
- Performance standards.

## Topic: Ethical Issues In International Relations And Funding

## Issues:
- Humanitarian intervention versus sovereignty.
- National interest versus global justice.
- Climate responsibility.
- Aid conditionality.
- Diaspora protection.
- Refugees and migration.
- Corporate and NGO funding transparency.
- Strategic autonomy.

## Answer tip:
- Link ethics with realism: moral claims must account for national interest, legality, reciprocity, and vulnerable populations.

## Topic: Corporate Governance

## Meaning:
- System by which companies are directed, controlled, and made accountable to shareholders and stakeholders.

## Ethical principles:
- Transparency.
- Board accountability.
- Minority shareholder protection.
- Audit integrity.
- Related-party transaction control.
- Consumer and worker responsibility.
- Environmental responsibility.

## Mains Pointers

- Use thinker names only with direct relevance.
- In public-service values answers, include institutional safeguards.
- For conscience questions, do not romanticize rule-breaking; show lawful channels.

- Ethical governance depends on transparency, accountability, responsiveness, and lawful discretion.
