## Quick Summary

- Attitude explains how people think, feel, and act toward an object, person, group, policy, or situation.
- Aptitude for civil service means the value-based capacity to use power lawfully, fairly, and compassionately.
- Emotional intelligence helps administrators handle stress, conflict, public anger, negotiation, and crisis without losing judgement.

## High-Yield Exam Notes

## Topic: Attitude

## Meaning:
- Attitude is a learned tendency to evaluate a person, object, issue, policy, or event positively or negatively.

## Components:
- Cognitive: beliefs and knowledge.
- Affective: feelings and emotions.
- Behavioural: action tendency.

## Example:
- Toward vaccination:
  - Cognitive: belief about safety and effectiveness.
  - Affective: fear or trust.
  - Behavioural: willingness or refusal.

## Topic: Functions Of Attitude

## Knowledge function:
- Helps simplify the world and organize information.

## Ego-defensive function:
- Protects self-image, sometimes through denial or prejudice.

## Value-expressive function:
- Expresses identity and principles.

## Utilitarian function:
- Helps gain rewards and avoid punishment.

## Social-adjustive function:
- Helps fit into a group.

## Topic: Attitude And Behaviour

## Relationship:
- Attitude influences behaviour, but behaviour also depends on law, incentives, peer pressure, institutional rules, and situational constraints.

## Why gap occurs:
- Fear.
- Social pressure.
- Lack of resources.
- Weak enforcement.
- Habit.
- Cognitive dissonance.
- Conflicting values.

## Administrative relevance:
- Public campaigns must change knowledge, emotion, and practical ability, not only provide information.

## Topic: Moral And Political Attitudes

## Moral attitude:
- Evaluation based on right and wrong, justice, duty, harm, fairness, dignity.

## Political attitude:
- Orientation toward state, authority, rights, ideology, parties, citizenship, policy, and institutions.

## Administrative caution:
- Civil servants can have private political views, but official action must be non-partisan, lawful, and evidence-based.

## Topic: Social Influence And Persuasion

## Social influence:
- Change in attitude or behaviour due to others.

## Types:
- Compliance: outward change due to reward or punishment.
- Identification: change to belong to a group.
- Internalization: genuine acceptance of a value.

## Persuasion tools:
- Credible messenger.
- Clear message.
- Repeated communication.
- Social proof.
- Incentives.
- Reducing barriers.
- Community participation.
- Emotional appeal with factual accuracy.

## Ethical persuasion:
- Transparent, truthful, non-coercive, respectful of dignity.

## Topic: Aptitude For Civil Service

## Meaning:
- Natural and cultivated suitability for public service, including judgement, values, temperament, and capability.

## Foundational values:
- Integrity.
- Impartiality.
- Non-partisanship.
- Objectivity.
- Dedication to public service.
- Empathy.
- Tolerance.
- Compassion toward weaker sections.

## Topic: Integrity

## Meaning:
- Consistency between values, words, decisions, and actions.

## Administrative example:
- Refusing to manipulate beneficiary lists despite pressure.

## Safeguards:
- Asset declaration.
- Recusal.
- Transparency.
- Written records.
- Audit.
- Conflict-of-interest rules.

## Topic: Impartiality And Non-Partisanship

## Meaning:
- Impartiality means fair treatment without bias.
- Non-partisanship means not using office to serve a party interest.

## Difference:
- Impartiality is about equal fairness among citizens.
- Non-partisanship is about neutrality toward political parties while obeying the elected government's lawful policy.

## Topic: Objectivity

## Meaning:
- Evidence-based decision-making using relevant facts, transparent criteria, and reason.

## Tools:
- Data.
- Public criteria.
- Independent verification.
- Peer review.
- Reasoned orders.
- Appeal mechanisms.

## Topic: Dedication To Public Service

## Meaning:
- Commitment to citizens, constitutional goals, service delivery, and public interest beyond personal convenience.

## Risks:
- Burnout.
- Misplaced zeal without legality.
- Heroic shortcuts.

## Balance:
- Dedication must be combined with law, teamwork, self-care, and institutional process.

## Topic: Empathy, Tolerance And Compassion

## Empathy:
- Understanding another person's situation and feelings.

## Tolerance:
- Respecting difference and disagreement within constitutional limits.

## Compassion:
- Active concern that motivates relief of suffering.

## Administrative example:
- Designing a welfare camp that accounts for disabled persons, elderly citizens, women, migrants, and digital exclusion.

## Topic: Emotional Intelligence

## Meaning:
- Ability to recognize, understand, manage, and use emotions constructively in oneself and others.

## Components:
- Self-awareness.
- Self-regulation.
- Motivation.
- Empathy.
- Social skills.

## Uses in administration:
- De-escalating conflict.
- Crisis communication.
- Handling public anger.
- Negotiating with stakeholders.
- Managing team stress.
- Preventing impulsive action.
- Building trust.

## Limits:
- EI cannot replace law, facts, or accountability.
- Emotional manipulation is unethical.

## Case application:
- During a protest, an emotionally intelligent officer listens, separates genuine grievances from unlawful actions, communicates clearly, protects vulnerable groups, avoids provocation, and documents decisions.
