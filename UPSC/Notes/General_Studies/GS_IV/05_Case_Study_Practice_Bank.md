## Case 1: Political Pressure In Welfare List

## Scenario:
- You are a district officer. A local elected representative pressures you to include ineligible names in a housing-beneficiary list and remove some eligible households who opposed him.

## Stakeholders:
- Eligible poor households.
- Ineligible beneficiaries.
- Elected representative.
- Department staff.
- Public exchequer.
- District administration.

## Values:
- Integrity.
- Impartiality.
- Rule of law.
- Compassion.
- Accountability.
- Public trust.

## Options:
- Accept the pressure.
- Reject verbally but make no record.
- Follow eligibility criteria, document pressure, create transparent review and grievance process.

## Best decision:
- Apply verified eligibility criteria, document reasons, allow appeals, brief senior authority if pressure persists, and publicly disclose final process as per rules.

## Long-term reforms:
- Digital list with public verification.
- Social audit.
- Gram sabha validation where applicable.
- Grievance window.

## Case 2: Disaster Relief Distribution Conflict

## Scenario:
- Flood relief is limited. Two villages are demanding immediate supplies. One is easier to reach; the other is more severely affected but access is difficult.

## Stakeholders:
- Flood victims.
- Vulnerable groups.
- Relief teams.
- Local administration.
- Health workers.

## Values:
- Compassion.
- Equity.
- Efficiency.
- Duty of care.
- Transparency.

## Decision:
- Prioritize severity and vulnerability, send immediate emergency supplies through available route, coordinate additional transport, communicate criteria transparently, and set up relief tracking.

## Safeguards:
- Public relief register.
- Medical triage.
- Women, children, elderly, disabled persons priority.
- Complaint desk.

## Case 3: Procurement Gift

## Scenario:
- A contractor bidding for a public project offers you an expensive gift before tender evaluation.

## Values:
- Integrity.
- Probity.
- Conflict of interest.
- Transparency.
- Fair competition.

## Decision:
- Refuse and report the gift as per conduct rules, document the incident, recuse if necessary, and ensure tender evaluation follows transparent criteria.

## Long-term reforms:
- E-procurement.
- Conflict-of-interest declarations.
- Tender committee records.
- Vendor communication protocol.

## Case 4: Data Manipulation For Target Achievement

## Scenario:
- Your superior asks you to inflate sanitation coverage data before a review meeting.

## Values:
- Truthfulness.
- Public interest.
- Accountability.
- Courage.
- Service delivery.

## Decision:
- Refuse data manipulation respectfully, provide accurate data with reasons for gaps, propose action plan, and document communication.

## Ethical reasoning:
- False data hides real beneficiaries' problems and leads to wrong resource allocation.

## Case 5: Communal Rumour On Social Media

## Scenario:
- A rumour on social media may trigger local violence. The information is unverified but spreading quickly.

## Values:
- Public order.
- Free speech.
- Responsibility.
- Restraint.
- Truth.

## Decision:
- Verify through police and local administration, issue factual public communication, engage community leaders, monitor lawfully, act against incitement under due process, and avoid blanket overreach unless legally justified.

## Case 6: Whistleblower Complaint

## Scenario:
- A junior employee reports corruption in procurement but fears retaliation.

## Values:
- Integrity.
- Protection of honest officials.
- Natural justice.
- Confidentiality.
- Accountability.

## Decision:
- Protect confidentiality, record complaint, initiate preliminary verification, prevent victimization, ensure fair hearing, and escalate to competent authority.

## Case 7: Development Versus Environment

## Scenario:
- A road project improves connectivity for remote villages but may damage a fragile forest corridor.

## Values:
- Development.
- Environmental protection.
- Livelihood.
- Intergenerational equity.
- Rule of law.

## Decision:
- Ensure legal environmental appraisal, examine alternatives, reduce alignment damage, include mitigation and wildlife passages, consult communities, and monitor compliance.

## Case 8: Digital Exclusion In Welfare Delivery

## Scenario:
- Biometric authentication failures are excluding elderly and disabled beneficiaries from food rations.

## Values:
- Dignity.
- Inclusion.
- Efficiency.
- Accountability.
- Compassion.

## Decision:
- Provide alternate authentication, manual exception register with audit, home delivery or assisted access for vulnerable persons, and technical grievance redressal.

## Prelims Pointers

- GS IV is Mains-focused, but case examples overlap with governance and social justice.

## Mains Pointers

- Never choose an option that violates law for convenience.
- Always include documentation, grievance redressal, and safeguards.
- Mention vulnerable groups explicitly.
- Prefer practical, institutionally defensible solutions.
