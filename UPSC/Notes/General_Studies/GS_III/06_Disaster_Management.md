## High-Yield Exam Notes

## Topic: Disaster

## Meaning:
- A serious disruption that causes loss of life, property, livelihood, infrastructure, environment, or services beyond the affected community's capacity to cope.

## Hazard, Vulnerability, Exposure And Risk:
- Hazard: potentially damaging event such as cyclone, earthquake, flood, landslide, drought, industrial accident.
- Exposure: people, assets, and systems located in hazard-prone areas.
- Vulnerability: susceptibility due to poverty, weak housing, age, disability, poor planning, environmental degradation.
- Capacity: ability to anticipate, resist, respond, and recover.
- Disaster risk: hazard x exposure x vulnerability, reduced by capacity.

## Topic: Disaster Management Cycle

## Stages:
- Prevention: avoid risk creation.
- Mitigation: reduce impact through structural and non-structural measures.
- Preparedness: plans, drills, early warning, stockpiles, training.
- Response: rescue, relief, emergency health, shelter, communication.
- Recovery: restore services, livelihoods, and institutions.
- Reconstruction: build back better with resilience.

## Important point:
- Disaster management should be integrated into development planning; otherwise roads, houses, schools, hospitals, dams, and urban systems can create new risks.

## Topic: Institutional Framework In India

## Key institutions:
- National Disaster Management Authority.
- State Disaster Management Authorities.
- District Disaster Management Authorities.
- National Disaster Response Force.
- State Disaster Response Forces.
- Indian Meteorological Department and other technical agencies.
- Local bodies and community institutions.

## Governance issues:
- Coordination across departments.
- State and local capacity.
- Last-mile warning.
- Relief distribution fairness.
- Urban planning enforcement.
- Data sharing.
- Post-disaster audit and learning.

## Topic: Early Warning Systems

## Meaning:
- Systems that detect hazards, forecast risk, communicate warnings, and trigger action.

## Components:
- Risk knowledge.
- Monitoring and forecasting.
- Communication.
- Response capability.

## Example:
- SACHET is an official disaster alert portal for warnings and preparedness information.

## Limitations:
- Warning not reaching vulnerable groups.
- Language and accessibility gaps.
- Last-mile connectivity.
- Warning fatigue.
- Lack of evacuation capacity.
- Poor trust in institutions.

## Topic: Floods

## Causes:
- Heavy rainfall.
- River overflow.
- Embankment failure.
- Urban drainage failure.
- Encroachment of floodplains.
- Dam mismanagement.
- Glacier/lake outburst risk in mountains.

## Measures:
- Floodplain zoning.
- Wetland protection.
- Forecasting.
- Reservoir operation protocols.
- Urban drainage.
- Nature-based solutions.
- Community preparedness.

## Topic: Cyclones

## Causes/features:
- Tropical ocean heat, low pressure, moisture, wind conditions.
- Coastal exposure and storm surges create high risk.

## Measures:
- Early warning.
- Cyclone shelters.
- Evacuation planning.
- Mangrove restoration.
- Coastal regulation.
- Resilient housing and infrastructure.
- Fisher communication.

## Topic: Earthquakes

## Causes:
- Tectonic movement and faulting.

## Risk factors:
- Unplanned urbanization.
- Non-engineered buildings.
- Poor enforcement of building codes.
- Hill-town vulnerability.

## Measures:
- Seismic microzonation.
- Building-code enforcement.
- Retrofitting.
- Public drills.
- Lifeline infrastructure resilience.

## Topic: Landslides

## Causes:
- Slope instability, heavy rain, earthquakes, road cutting, deforestation, poor drainage, construction.

## Measures:
- Hazard zonation.
- Slope stabilization.
- Drainage.
- Regulated construction.
- Vegetation.
- Early warning.

## Topic: Drought

## Causes:
- Rainfall deficit.
- Groundwater depletion.
- Crop-water mismatch.
- Land degradation.

## Measures:
- Watershed management.
- Drought-resistant crops.
- Crop insurance.
- Water budgeting.
- Livelihood diversification.
- Fodder banks.

## Topic: Heatwaves

## Why important:
- Rising heat affects health, labour productivity, agriculture, electricity demand, and urban poor.

## Measures:
- Heat action plans.
- Early warning.
- Work-hour adjustment.
- Cooling shelters.
- Urban greening.
- Drinking-water access.
- Public health readiness.

## Topic: Industrial And Chemical Disasters

## Causes:
- Poor safety systems.
- Hazardous material leakage.
- Weak inspection.
- Human error.
- Urban encroachment around hazardous sites.

## Measures:
- Safety audits.
- Emergency plans.
- Community right-to-know.
- Hazard mapping.
- Liability and compensation.
- Trained first responders.

## Topic: Build Back Better

## Meaning:
- Reconstruction should reduce future risk instead of restoring the same vulnerability.

## Components:
- Resilient housing.
- Stronger lifeline infrastructure.
- Livelihood recovery.
- Ecosystem restoration.
- Social inclusion.
- Insurance and financial resilience.
- Community participation.

## Prelims Pointers

- Know hazard types, disaster-management cycle, NDMA/NDRF roles, early warning, cyclone/flood/earthquake terms.
- Map disaster-prone regions: Himalayas, coasts, floodplains, drought-prone regions, seismic zones.

## Mains Pointers

- Draw the disaster-management cycle.
- Use "risk = hazard x exposure x vulnerability / capacity" as a conceptual tool.
- Always include vulnerable groups: women, children, elderly, disabled persons, migrants, poor households.
- Mention local bodies and community preparedness.
