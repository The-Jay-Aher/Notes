## Quick Summary

- UPSC science and technology is application-oriented, not a specialist engineering paper.
- Study what a technology does, where India stands, how it affects society, economy, governance, security, ethics, and regulation.
- Use official sources for Indian achievements and current missions; avoid unverified hype.

## High-Yield Exam Notes

## Topic: Science And Technology In Everyday Life

## Meaning:
- Scientific knowledge and technological applications affecting health, communication, agriculture, education, transport, finance, governance, environment, and security.

## Examples:
- Digital payments.
- Vaccines.
- Telemedicine.
- Remote sensing.
- Weather forecasting.
- Precision agriculture.
- Renewable energy.
- Water purification.
- Assistive technologies.
- Online education.

## Answer framework:
1. Define the technology.
2. Explain the application.
3. Show benefits.
4. Identify risks or exclusions.
5. Add governance and regulation.
6. Conclude with responsible innovation.

## Topic: Indigenization Of Technology

## Meaning:
- Developing, adapting, manufacturing, and maintaining strategic or high-value technologies domestically.

## Importance:
- Strategic autonomy.
- Supply-chain resilience.
- Defence preparedness.
- Export potential.
- Skilled jobs.
- Lower import dependence.
- Technology spillovers.

## Challenges:
- R&D funding gaps.
- Academia-industry disconnect.
- Import dependence for components.
- Scale and quality constraints.
- Patent and standards ecosystem.
- Procurement delays.
- Talent retention.

## Way forward:
- Mission-mode R&D.
- Public procurement for innovation.
- Industry-academia collaboration.
- Testing and certification labs.
- Patient capital.
- IP support.
- Standards participation.
- International collaboration without dependence.

## Topic: Information Technology And Digital Governance

## Key Areas:
- Digital public infrastructure.
- Cloud computing.
- Artificial intelligence.
- Cyber security.
- Data governance.
- Digital identity.
- Digital payments.
- Service-delivery platforms.

## Benefits:
- Faster service delivery.
- Reduced leakage.
- Data-driven policy.
- Financial inclusion.

## Risks:

## Topic: Intellectual Property Rights

## Meaning:
- Legal rights protecting creations of the mind and enabling creators or owners to benefit from commercial use.

## Major IPR categories:
- Patents.
- Designs.
- Trademarks.
- Copyright.
- Geographical indications.
- Semiconductor integrated circuit layout designs.
- Trade secrets or undisclosed information.

## Why IPR matters:
- Encourages innovation.
- Supports technology transfer.
- Protects branding and creativity.
- Attracts investment.
- Can affect access to medicines, seeds, and knowledge.

## Balance needed:
- Reward innovation without blocking public interest.
- Use compulsory licensing and flexibilities where lawful and justified.
- Protect traditional knowledge and geographical indications.
- Strengthen patent quality and examination.

## Prelims Pointers

- Know basic definitions of patent, copyright, trademark, GI, design, trade secret.
- Track major Indian space missions from ISRO official releases.
- Science Prelims often asks applications more than formulas.

## Mains Pointers

- Do not write only technical details.
- For every technology, prepare benefits, risks, regulation, ethical issues, and India-specific capacity.
- Use diagrams for AI governance, biotech risk, space applications, and IPR balance.
