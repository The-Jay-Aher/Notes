- Prepare it as a skills paper: reading accuracy, reasoning, decision-making, numeracy, and data interpretation.

## High-Yield Exam Notes

## Concept: Comprehension

## Meaning:
- Reading a passage for central idea, inference, author position, and logical flow.

## Why important:
- It consumes time and punishes careless assumptions.

## Example:
- If the passage says a policy can help but has implementation limits, avoid extreme answers such as "the author fully supports the policy without reservation."

## Possible question:
- Which statement best captures the author's main argument?

## Concept: Logical Reasoning

## Meaning:
- Testing whether conclusions follow from given information.

## Why important:
- UPSC uses familiar reasoning ideas but often with time pressure and dense wording.

## Example:
- Statement-conclusion, assumption, strengthen/weaken, seating, direction, blood relation, ranking, and syllogism questions.

## Possible question:
- Which assumption is necessary for the argument to hold?

## Concept: Decision Making And Problem Solving

## Meaning:
- Choosing a balanced, lawful, ethical, and practical response under constraints.

## Why important:
- It tests administrative temperament rather than memorised facts.

## Example:
- In a disaster-relief conflict, prioritise life, legality, coordination, transparency, and vulnerable groups.

## Possible question:
- Which response is most appropriate for a public official facing conflicting stakeholder demands?

## Concept: Numeracy

## Meaning:
- Class X arithmetic used in time-bound problem solving.

## Why important:
- Basic arithmetic mistakes can waste marks even when concepts are easy.

## Example:
- Percentages, profit-loss, ratios, averages, mixtures, time-work, time-distance, simple/compound interest, permutations basics.

## Concept: Data Interpretation

## Meaning:
- Reading tables, charts, and graphs to calculate or infer values.

## Why important:
- Often combines arithmetic with attention to units, base years, percentages, and approximations.

## Example:
- A bar chart gives production while a table gives percentage export; the answer needs both.

## Prelims Pointers

- CSAT is qualifying but compulsory.
- Practise with timed sets; untimed comfort is misleading.
- Keep an error log for calculation mistakes, misread conditions, and over-inference in passages.
- Do not spend too long on one puzzle.
- Attempt comprehension with evidence from the passage, not outside knowledge.

## Mains Pointers

- CSAT decision-making practice helps GS IV case studies.
- Comprehension practice improves Essay and Mains answer clarity.

## Keywords for Answer Writing

- Comprehension
- Inference
- Assumption
- Data sufficiency
- Administrative judgement
- Time management

## Possible UPSC Questions

- Prelims-style: Find the valid conclusion from the given statements.
- Prelims-style: Interpret a table and identify the correct percentage change.
- Prelims-style: Choose the best decision in a public-service scenario.
- Mains-style linkage: Explain why decision-making questions are related to civil-service ethics.

## Revision Summary

- CSAT requires weekly practice even though it is qualifying.
- The minimum qualifying mark is 33 percent.
- Build speed in arithmetic and restraint in comprehension.
- Solve previous-year papers to understand difficulty and time pressure.
- Use CSAT practice to improve GS IV case-study judgement.
