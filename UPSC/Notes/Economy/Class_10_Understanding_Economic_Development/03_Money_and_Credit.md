## High-Yield Exam Notes

- Money solves the problem of double coincidence of wants by acting as a commonly accepted medium of exchange.
- Modern money includes currency and demand deposits; currency has legal acceptance and banking deposits support cheque/digital payments.
- Banks keep a fraction of deposits as cash and lend the rest, linking savers with borrowers.
- Credit can raise production and income when terms are fair, but can create a debt trap when interest/collateral terms are exploitative.
- Terms of credit include interest rate, collateral, documentation, repayment schedule, and lender conditions.
- Formal credit includes banks and cooperatives; informal credit includes moneylenders, traders, employers, relatives, and friends.
- RBI supervises formal credit institutions; NABARD and SHGs are important for rural and poor borrowers.
- UPSC angle: financial inclusion, digital payments, rural credit, SHGs, debt trap, formalisation of credit.
