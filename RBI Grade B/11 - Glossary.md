# 11 - RBI Grade B Glossary

## Purpose

Practical glossary for RBI Grade B preparation.

## Terms

### Monetary Policy

RBI's policy framework for influencing inflation, liquidity, credit conditions, and economic activity.

### Repo Rate

Policy rate at which RBI lends short-term funds to banks against eligible securities, subject to the current operating framework.

### Monetary Transmission

Process through which policy changes affect bank rates, market rates, credit, demand, and inflation.

### Inflation Targeting

Policy framework focused on keeping inflation around a target range as defined by the official framework.

### Financial Inclusion

Access to useful and affordable financial services such as accounts, payments, credit, insurance, pensions, and remittances.

### NPA

Non-performing asset. A stressed loan where repayment is overdue beyond the regulatory threshold.

### Capital Adequacy

Bank capital buffer maintained against risk-weighted assets.

### ESI

Economic and Social Issues: development, poverty, growth, inflation, social sectors, schemes, and current reports.

### Finance and Management

Phase II domain covering financial systems and management concepts.

## Small Details That Matter Later

- Terms should be linked to institutions and mechanisms.
- RBI reports are high-value sources.
- Current rates and thresholds must be verified close to the exam.
- Financial terms often appear in both MCQ and descriptive forms.
