# July Week 3 (13/07 - 19/07)

## 13/07

### 10th July - National Fist Farmers Day

### MoEF&CC Inaugurates CoE on Human-Wildlife Conflict in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu

Union Minister for Environment, Forest and Climate Change, **Bhupender Yadav**, inaugurated a **Centre of Excellence (CoE) on Human-Wildlife Conflict** at the Wildlife Institute of India campus in **Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu**.

- The CoE is intended to act as a **national hub** for scientific and evidence-based conflict management.
- A **National Human-Wildlife Conflict Portal** was launched for data management, knowledge sharing, and decision support.

### QCI and NSIC Sign MoU to Improve MSME Quality and Market Access

Quality Council of India (QCI) and National Small Industries Corporation (NSIC) signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to improve quality standards, competitiveness, and market access for MSMEs.

- QCI will provide technical support on quality standards, accreditation, and certification to the NSIC MSME ecosystem.
- A **Joint Coordination Committee (JCC)** will meet **quarterly** during the **5-year MoU** period to monitor implementation.
- The collaboration aligns MSME support frameworks such as **ZED (Zero Defect Zero Effect)** and **NSIC's Single Point Registration Scheme (SPRS)**.

### ICMR to Set Up India's First High-Altitude Medicine Centre in Himachal Pradesh

The Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) laid the foundation for a centre focused on high-altitude medicine and public health research at Keylong, Lahaul and Spiti, Himachal Pradesh.

- It is positioned as **India's first dedicated institution** for high-altitude medicine and public health in such terrain.
- Research focus includes high-altitude populations, tribal health, disaster preparedness, digital health innovation, and healthcare delivery in remote regions.
- ICMR is India's apex body for formulation, coordination, and promotion of biomedical research (**Director General: Dr Rajiv Bahl**).
- The event was inaugurated by **Union Health Minister Jagat Prakash Nadda**.
### Government Mandates TReDS Routing of MSME Invoices by CPSEs

The government directed **all operating Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs)** to route invoices for goods and services procured from MSMEs through **TReDS (Trade Receivables Discounting System)** platforms authorized by **RBI**.

- This implements a key Union Budget announcement for **FY 2026-27**.
- CPSEs are required to disclose invoice routing and settlement details through **TReDS**.
- CPSEs must also obtain **statutory auditor certification** on TReDS registration and compliance during annual audit.
- The objective is to improve MSME cash flow by enabling **collateral-free invoice discounting** before payment due dates.

About TReDS:

- TReDS is an **RBI-authorized** digital framework (**operational since 2017**) for financing and discounting MSME receivables from corporates, government departments, and PSUs via competitive bidding by financiers.
### RBI Launches Three Surveys for Monetary Policy Inputs

RBI launched three key household surveys:

1. Inflation Expectations Survey of Households (IESH)
2. Rural Consumer Confidence Survey (RCCS)
3. Urban Consumer Confidence Survey (UCCS)

These surveys gather household feedback on inflation, economic conditions, employment, and income across **19 cities and 31 States/UTs**.

- The results feed into monetary policy assessment ahead of the **MPC meeting (3-5 August 2026)**.

About the three surveys:

- **IESH:** Conducted across 19 cities; captures qualitative inflation expectations and quantitative inflation estimates for current period, 3 months ahead, and 1 year ahead.
- **RCCS:** Captures rural and semi-urban perceptions and 1-year-ahead expectations on economy, employment, prices, income, and spending in 31 States/UTs.
- **UCCS:** Conducted across 19 cities; records qualitative urban household views on economy, employment, prices, income, and spending for current period and 1 year ahead.

### World Bank Approves USD 890 Million for India's Rooftop Solar Programme

The World Bank approved **USD 890 million** for **PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana**, aimed at scaling rooftop solar adoption among households.

- Funding composition:
  - **USD 820 million** from IBRD (International Bank for Reconstruction and Development)
  - **USD 60 million** concessional finance from the Clean Technology Fund (CTF)
  - **USD 10 million** grant from the IBRD Livable Planet Fund
- The package aims to support rooftop deployment, domestic manufacturing, and private investment mobilization.
- The World Bank also expects to crowd in around **USD 4.2 billion** in private financing via commercial channels.

### GoI Approves Dixon-Vivo JV for Domestic Smartphone Manufacturing

The Government of India approved a Joint Venture (JV) between Dixon Technologies (India) Ltd and Vivo Mobile India Ltd for manufacturing electronic devices, especially smartphones, in India.

- The JV structure is reported as **51% (Dixon) and 49% (Vivo)**.
- It is positioned to operate as an original equipment manufacturer (OEM).
- Reported paid-up capital contribution is **Rs 5 crore from each side**.
- The approval allows Vivo Mobile India to participate under **Press Note 3 (2020)**, which requires prior government approval for investment from countries sharing a land border with India.
